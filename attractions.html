Attractions

Soak up Ireland!

What better way to rest your bones off the pitching than seeing a country! Ireland is a playground for those wanting to experience natural beauty, cultural centers, music, and of course, the "craic" (how Irish people describe fun).

If the below images of Ireland don't get you excited about the tour, we don't know what will!

Cork

Known as the "real capital" of Ireland to Corkonians, this is Ireland's second largest city. Cork has a proud tradition of rugby, prodcuing some of the best players in Ireland, including Ronan O'Gara.

Galway

Galway is a vibrant city in the West of Ireland. Known for its arts and music, Galway attracts people from all over the world. It has a young population and there will never be a dull moment on your visit here. Sit outside one of the pubs like a local and watch the world go by.

Dublin

The hustle and bustle of our Capital draws millions in to the city every year. Dublin is best seen by foot - walk around the pedestrianised streets and see the locals relax in the many cafes and pubs. The famous Guinness Storehouse (pictured) is located in the heart of the city.

Rugby Match

Brace yourselves for a clash of top class athletes, as we will be taking you to a professional rugby match! There really is nothing like seeing the game live.

Cliffs of Moher

These magnificent cliffs are a must see when you come to Ireland. Pictures can't really show just how jaw-dropping the cliffs are - but don't worry, we will visit them on our tour! They have been featured in films by legends such as Martin Scorcese and the Harry Potter franchise.

Glendalough

Glendalough means "Valley of the two lakes", and is home to a 6th century Monastic settlement. See how the monks lived all that time ago in the stunning surroundings of the Glendalough Glacier Valley.

Rock of Cashel

This remarkable group of medival buildings dates back to the 12th century and have survived war and famine, thanks to them being elevated and walled off from outsiders. This is a great place to reflect on a defence strategy for your next match!
