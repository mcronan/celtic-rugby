Spring Break

Thursday 16th March DUBLIN

Céad míle Fáilte (One hundred thousand welcomes) as you take your first steps on Irish soil! The team will be met at the airport and taken on a panoramic tour of Dublin City before heading to the hotel to check-in. After showering the team will be brought to the Aviva Stadium, where they will receive a private guided tour of the home of Irish Rugby, and learn about its 150 year history. A hearty welcoming dinner will be ready back at the hotel for the team to set them up for the week ahead!

Friday 17th March DUBLIN

Game day! This will be the first of three challenges the team will face. After an early breakfast the team will head out for their game against a local Dublin high school/club team. After the match the team will head into the city for a well earned tour of the Guinness Storehouse. It is St. Patrick’s Day, so the celebrations will be in full swing. We will organise an outing to show off this special event.

Saturday 18th March CORK

Early departure today - it is down to Glendalough to see Ireland’s most beautiful glacial valley and a 6th century monastic settlement. Following Glendalough it is time to hit the road, stopping at Clonmel to enjoy lunch, and to view the Rock of Cashel. The last stop today is Blarney, where the team will see the Blarney Castle and kiss the Blarney Stone. The team will then check in to the hotel and have a team dinner.

Sunday 19th March CORK

Game day! Time to suit up for the second game of the trip against a local Cork high school/club rugby team. Afterwards the team will be encouraged to explore Cork city on a panoramic bus tour. The team will be given the afternoon to explore the city. Cork is a wonderful city, and we will be making recommendations on how to fully enjoy the city. We will then head back to the hotel on the bus for some rest.

Monday 20th March GALWAY

The team will depart Cork and make their way up to the West of Ireland to see the world famous Cliffs of Moher. We will then stop in a small seaside town to enjoy a light lunch, before heading up to Galway. Upon arriving in Galway they team will receive a panoramic bus tour of Galway city and then check into the hotel. A team dinner will be provided tonight at the hotel.

Tuesday 21st March GALWAY

The team will be coached by a Connaught professional rugby player, to learn how the game is played in Europe and pick up valuable tips and advice! This will help the team prepare for the next game, and will be of huge benefit to the team in the future. After training, and if the team is brave enough, they head to Salthill to jump of the Blackrock Diving Tower - the cold water of Galway Bay will help their muscles relax before the game tomorrow! The team will be encourages to explore Galway town to see some of its famous medieval streets and shops for the evening.

Wednesday 22nd March GALWAY

Final game day! Today the team will play a Galway high school/club team in the morning, and put what they learned from the professional’s training to the test. Afterwards the team will spend the evening at their leisure. We will provide a farewell dinner in Galway town that evening, so the team can bond and share stories of the hectic week that just went by!

Thursday 23rd March

It’s all over! The team will be brought to Dublin Airport and fly home, ready for the rugby challenges that lie ahead!
